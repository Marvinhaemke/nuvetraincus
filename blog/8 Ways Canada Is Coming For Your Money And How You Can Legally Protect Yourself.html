8 Ways Canada Is Coming For Your Money And How You Can Legally Protect Yourself

Author: Dr. Robin (CEO, Global Sovereign)

Published: 11th of December 2025  |  Topic: Asset Protection, Tax Planning, Offshore Structuring, Wealth Preservation

Canada is becoming more aggressive every single year when it comes to taking money from its most successful residents. Entrepreneurs are feeling cornered because the system is designed to extract, restrict and control.

In this article you will find the eight biggest financial threats for Canadian entrepreneurs and how international structuring can protect your wealth.

1. Double Taxation On US Income

Elena from Ottawa invested in a Florida property held in a US LLC. The investment performed well until she wanted to bring the money back to Canada. The CRA classified the LLC as a corporation and taxed the income again as a dividend. In the end, almost 80 percent of her profits were gone through double taxation.

The solution would have been clear from the start: a US Limited Partnership. The income flows through directly for tax purposes, allowing foreign tax credits and avoiding double taxation.

Anyone earning money in the United States should not use an LLC. Canadian investors need an LP or a cross border structure to avoid CRA double taxation.

2. Business Failure Still Destroys Personal Wealth In Canada

Jamal, a café owner from Ottawa, believed his Canadian corporation would protect him. When COVID hit and the business collapsed, he discovered that his personal wealth was not truly separated. Creditors came after him personally. Home, savings, retirement funds, everything gone. And the CRA took its share first.

A US offshore trust structure would have separated his personal wealth cleanly from his business risk. In Canada, a normal corporation simply is not enough to shield assets.

Canadian corporations offer very little real asset protection. International asset protection structures are necessary when you carry risk.

3. Probate, Forced Heirship And Endless Paperwork

The Lefebvre family in Quebec experienced the complete opposite of what Jean wanted for his wife and children. A five-million-dollar estate, but instead of security, the family faced 18 months of court delays, one hundred thousand dollars in legal fees and the intervention of forced heirship rules in Quebec. Jean’s own will was practically irrelevant.

A US cross border trust would have avoided probate entirely, transferred the assets privately and immediately, and bypassed forced heirship.

Anyone who does not want courts and provincial laws to decide what happens to their estate needs a cross border trust solution.

4. Hidden US Estate Taxes On Canadians

The Thompson father owned American stocks worth 1.2 million dollars. After his death, the family was shocked. The IRS demanded about 500,000 dollars in estate tax because Canadians only receive a tiny exemption of 60,000 dollars.

With an international trust structure, they could have transferred the assets in a tax-optimized way and legally reduced the tax burden.

Every Canadian with US stocks, ETFs or real estate risks paying up to 40 percent US estate tax. Without structuring, it becomes extremely expensive.

5. Currency Losses And Bank Fees Quietly Stealing Wealth

Raj from Vancouver exported goods to the United States. When the Canadian dollar dropped six percent in 2023 and the bank charged three percent conversion fees, he lost more than fifty thousand dollars. These silent losses destroyed his margins and peace of mind.

He created a US structure, opened a US bank account and held his earnings directly in US dollars. No more fees, no more sleepless nights.

Anyone earning US dollars should not hold them in Canada. A US structure and a US account stop exchange rate losses and bank fees.

6. Government Bank Freezes With No Warning

The events of the Emergencies Act in 2022 hit many people hard. More than two hundred Canadians, including Tamara Lich, suddenly had no access to their bank accounts. No warning, no transparency, no right to use their own money.

A US bank account outside of Canadian jurisdiction remains accessible even if Canada freezes accounts.

Political risks in Canada are real. International banking solutions are a financial safety net for entrepreneurs and families.

7. Losing Global Deals Because Of Entity Mismatch

Alex, a tech founder from Toronto, lost a one-million-dollar investment because his Canadian business structure did not fit the requirements of the US private equity investor. The deal collapsed and the opportunity was gone.

With a US corporation or an offshore company, he would have received the financing immediately.

Canadian business structures often do not match global investor requirements. International structuring opens doors to deals, partnerships and capital.

8. Geopolitical Shocks Destroying Wealth Overnight

Carlos ran a successful auto supply business. When a new US tariff of 25 percent hit his goods in 2025, more than one million dollars of expected income was at risk. On top of that, new Canadian regulations and discussions about wealth taxes increased the pressure.

He used an international structure and moved five million dollars in assets to Mexico. This allowed him to avoid massive financial damage.

One country alone is not a safe haven. International diversification protects wealth from political and economic shocks.
